Nao textbook: Rules for clauses

Clause is a special type of a top-word. It opens a whole new subsentence with its owns predicates, top-adverbs, etc.
When following a top-noun or a top-adjective, it modifies the top-noun. In other cases it works as a normal noun, becoming either the subject or the object.

Special prepositions that open a clause: hoi=because(of), hoic=resulting in, keo=but, keoc=although, sei=if, seic=then.
When they open a clause, it should be thought as if there was a hidden pe right after them.

pe clause

If inside a pe clause there is the wh-question word ke=what, the whole construction works like a function returning the answer to that question. For example, we have the wh-question mic tuo pike="where do you work?". Now if we put pe before it, it becomes pe mic tuo pike="the place where you work".
